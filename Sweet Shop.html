    A confectionery store (more commonly referred to as a sweet shop in the United Kingdom, a candy shop or candy store in North America, or a lolly shop[1] in Australia and New Zealand) sells confectionery and the intended market is usually children. Most confectionery stores are filled with an assortment of sweets far larger than a grocer or convenience store could accommodate. They often offer a selection of old-fashioned treats and sweets from different countries. Very often unchanged in layout since their inception, confectioneries are known for their warming and nostalgic feel. A sweet shop is a store that specializes in selling sweets, candies, and other confectionery items. Sweet shops often offer a wide variety of candies, chocolates, gummies, lollipops, chewing gum, fudges, toffees, and other treats. They may also offer gift baskets or boxes of sweets that can be customized for special occasions such as weddings, birthdays, or holidays. Some sweet shops make their own candies and chocolates in-house, using traditional recipes and methods. These shops may also offer classes or workshops where customers can learn to make their own confectionery treats. Sweet shops have been around for centuries, with some of the oldest sweet shops dating back to the 1700s. Sweet shops were especially popular in the 19th and early 20th centuries, when they were often located near theaters and other entertainment venues. Today, sweet shops can be found in many countries around the world, and they continue to be a popular destination for people of all ages who enjoy sweet treats. Many sweet shops also have an online presence, making it easy for customers to order their favorite candies and chocolates from the comfort of their own homes.
